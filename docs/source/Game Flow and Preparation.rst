==================================
Game Flow and Table Preparation
==================================

The Structure of Play
---------------------
A standard table requires **4 players**[cite: 1056]. A full game consists of **four rounds**, named sequentially after the four cardinal winds: **East (東)**, **South (南)**, **West (西)**, and **North (北)**[cite: 1061, 1063].

* A single round is complete when the role of Dealer rotates fully around the table[cite: 1062].
* If the Dealer wins a hand or if the hand concludes in a scoreless draw, the Dealer retains their position, extending the length of that round[cite: 1060, 1062].
* If any of the three non-dealers wins a hand, the role of Dealer passes immediately to the player sitting on the current Dealer's right[cite: 1059].
* Once all four rounds are finished, or a pre-determined end point is reached, the player holding the most chips or points wins[cite: 1064, 1065].

Determining the Initial Dealer
------------------------------
To establish table positions at the start of a game, Balai Hub recognizes a few standard options[cite: 1071]:

1. **Age:** Automatically assign the role to the youngest player at the table[cite: 1072].
2. **Dice Roll:** Every player rolls the dice; the individual with the highest total is selected[cite: 1073].
3. **Wind Shuffle:** Take one East, South, West, and North tile, mix them face down, and have each player draw one[cite: 1074]. The player drawing East becomes the Initial Dealer and sits in position 1[cite: 1075].

The Initial Dealer positions a corner triangle marker (the *starting indicator*) on their edge of the table and sets the **Round Wind Indicator** to East (東)[cite: 1081].

Step-by-Step Preparation Phase
------------------------------

Step 1: Wash the Tiles
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
All 144 tiles are flipped completely face down on the center of the table and mixed thoroughly by all players[cite: 778]. This is referred to as "washing" the tiles[cite: 777].

Step 2: Build the Wall
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
Every player gathers tiles to construct a double-layered wall along their table boundary[cite: 779]:
* Each individual wall must be **two rows high and 18 tiles wide** (36 tiles total per wall)[cite: 779, 780].
* The walls are then pushed slightly inward to interface at the corners, forming an interlocking square pinwheel[cite: 781].

Step 3: Break the Wall
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
1. The active Dealer rolls three dice[cite: 782].
2. Counting counter-clockwise around the table starting with their own wall as #1, the Dealer counts up to the number rolled to determine which player's wall will be opened[cite: 783, 784].
3. The player whose wall was selected counts that exact number of tile columns from **right to left**[cite: 803].
4. They separate the wall cleanly after that column[cite: 804]. This split defines the **Live Wall** (where players draw standard turn tiles) and the **Dead Wall** (the short end stack used purely for replacement tiles)[cite: 806].

.. image:: images/breaking_the_wall.jpg
   :alt: Example illustration demonstrating a wall break
   :align: center

Step 4: Drawing the Starting Hands
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
* The Dealer draws the very first 2x2 block (4 tiles total) right after the split on the Live Wall[cite: 806].
* Moving counter-clockwise, the next player draws the subsequent 2x2 block, and so forth, until all players have gone around 3 times and hold 12 tiles each[cite: 806, 807].
* The Dealer then draws 1 more tile from the Live Wall, and each player draws 1 single tile in sequence until everyone holds **13 tiles**[cite: 808, 809].
* Finally, the Dealer draws **one additional 14th tile** to form their initial starting hand[cite: 810]. Players may now lift and review their hands[cite: 810].

Step 5: Organize Tiles & Declare Flowers
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
* Players sort their hands by suits and numerical sequences for easier tracking[cite: 811].
* Starting with the Dealer, any player holding bonus Flower or Season tiles must lay them face up on the table next to their hand[cite: 811].
* They immediately replace them by drawing an equal number of tiles from the back of the **Dead Wall**[cite: 811].
* If a replacement tile happens to be another Flower, they repeat the process until all players have clean, flower-free starting hands[cite: 811].
