==================================
Game Flow and Table Preparation
==================================

The Structure of Play
---------------------
A standard table requires **4 players**. A full game consists of **four rounds**, named sequentially after the four cardinal winds: **East (東)**, **South (南)**, **West (西)**, and **North (北)**.

* A single round is complete when the role of Dealer rotates fully around the table.
* If the Dealer wins a hand or if the hand concludes in a scoreless draw, the Dealer retains their position, extending the length of that round.
* If any of the three non-dealers wins a hand, the role of Dealer passes immediately to the player sitting on the current Dealer's right.
* Once all four rounds are finished, or a pre-determined end point is reached, the player holding the most chips or points wins.

Determining the Initial Dealer
------------------------------
To establish table positions at the start of a game, Balai Hub recognizes a few standard options:

1. **Age:** Automatically assign the role to the youngest player at the table.
2. **Dice Roll:** Every player rolls the dice; the individual with the highest total is selected.
3. **Wind Shuffle:** Take one East, South, West, and North tile, mix them face down, and have each player draw one. The player drawing East becomes the Initial Dealer and sits in position 1.

The Initial Dealer positions a corner triangle marker (the *starting indicator*) on their edge of the table and sets the **Round Wind Indicator** to East (東).

Step-by-Step Preparation Phase
------------------------------

Step 1: Wash the Tiles
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
All 144 tiles are flipped completely face down on the center of the table and mixed thoroughly by all players. This is referred to as "washing" the tiles.

Step 2: Build the Wall
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
Every player gathers tiles to construct a double-layered wall along their table boundary:
* Each individual wall must be **two rows high and 18 tiles wide** (36 tiles total per wall).
* The walls are then pushed slightly inward to interface at the corners, forming an interlocking square pinwheel.

Step 3: Break the Wall
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
1. The active Dealer rolls three dice.
2. Counting counter-clockwise around the table starting with their own wall as #1, the Dealer counts up to the number rolled to determine which player's wall will be opened.
3. The player whose wall was selected counts that exact number of tile columns from **right to left**.
4. They separate the wall cleanly after that column. This split defines the **Live Wall** (where players draw standard turn tiles) and the **Dead Wall** (the short end stack used purely for replacement tiles).

.. image:: images/breaking_the_wall.jpg
   :alt: Example illustration demonstrating a wall break
   :align: center

Step 4: Drawing the Starting Hands
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
* The Dealer draws the very first 2x2 block (4 tiles total) right after the split on the Live Wall.
* Moving counter-clockwise, the next player draws the subsequent 2x2 block, and so forth, until all players have gone around 3 times and hold 12 tiles each.
* The Dealer then draws 1 more tile from the Live Wall, and each player draws 1 single tile in sequence until everyone holds **13 tiles**.
* Finally, the Dealer draws **one additional 14th tile** to form their initial starting hand. Players may now lift and review their hands.

Step 5: Organize Tiles & Declare Flowers
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
* Players sort their hands by suits and numerical sequences for easier tracking.
* Starting with the Dealer, any player holding bonus Flower or Season tiles must lay them face up on the table next to their hand.
* They immediately replace them by drawing an equal number of tiles from the back of the **Dead Wall**.
* If a replacement tile happens to be another Flower, they repeat the process until all players have clean, flower-free starting hands.
